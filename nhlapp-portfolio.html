NHL 20 Companion App

This is a personal UI/UX design project I created based on the Ultimate Team mode in NHL 20. This mode had a companion app similar to this back in NHL 14, but it was removed for various reasons. Other EA Sports titles have recently been given a new companion app, leading me to design one for NHL. I focused on keeping it simple in order for the app to run smoothly on mobile devices, but including a full range of features that are useful to an Ultimate Team player during gameplay or away from their console. This app would be beneficial to both the players, letting them manage their Ultimate Team account from anywhere, as well as EA Sports. The ability to buy points and packs from anywhere with an internet connection would lead to a higher number of points purchased and packs sold.

The UX Explained

The UX for this application was focused on speed. The UI doesn't use many photographs, except for essential elements such as the card and pack images. The layout focuses on allowing the user to get to any page with ease, simply sliding out the menu from the side. All interactions are intuitive touch actions, allowing users to open information on cards with a tap, or view the current packs with a slide.

The UI Explained

In the theme of the app I used the purple to pink gradient featured on the standard cover for the game. You can see each individual page I created in the gallery below. When creating the pages for the animation I focused on which pages would showcase the best features of the app. I included a tab for the core activities an Ultimate Team player would engage in apart from actually playing the game. A lot of the UI elements are based on the visuals in the actual game, such as the player stats page and pack icons.
